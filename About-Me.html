About Me:

I am a self-taught artist, born and raised in central Minnesota.

I started with a strong appreciation for the masters of Europe,  Leonardo Da Vinci and the Pre-Raphaelites, which encouraged an attention to detail and subtle use of color in lighting and atmosphere.
Then through the Impressionists and Post-Impressionists, I discovered Ukiyo-e of Japan such as Katsushika Hokosai and Kawase Hasui, which seemed to depict as accuracy of feeling and time through form and color as the impressionists could. I also take note of the dreaminess of the surrealists.

I consider myself a student of Van Gogh, having developed a style that emphasizes color and form as the main channels of communication.

I work mainly in oil paint, pen and ink, and colored pencil.
I am able to do UI design and am learning web development a little everyday.

For fun, I also do pixel art and animation, music production for nearly 20 years, and am learning game development in the Godot Engine for a central location to put all my creative work so I don't have to abandon one passion for another.
